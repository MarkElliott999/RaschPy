Bivector estimator (#9)
-----------------------
Investigation of weak threshold parameter recovery in bivector model.

Simulation comparison across 11 conditions (20 reps each) showed:
  - Items r:     marginal 0.899  vs  direct 0.904  (approx equal)
  - Threshold r: marginal 0.671  vs  direct 0.797  (+0.126 mean, direct wins uniformly)

Large-scale simulation (87,481 rows, varying rater range, item range, missing rate):
  - Direct wins in 72.9% of replications, mean Pearson advantage +0.029
  - Advantage largest at low item range, diminishes as item range increases
  - No bias — threshold estimates unbiased, residual error is pure variance

Direct estimator = items model + thresholds model run simultaneously, with correction:
  - facet_effects_bivector_items: identical to items model (PAIR summed over thresholds)
  - facet_effects_bivector_thresholds: thresholds model PAIR (summed over items),
    minus per-rater mean item effect mu_r, then zero-summed per rater
  This is the natural decomposition: items and thresholds models jointly, with
  threshold estimates deconfounded for overall rater severity using item estimates.

matrix_marginals=False is now the default. matrix_marginals=True retains the
marginal-means approach (full matrix PAIR -> marginal means).
